About

it is i, the frenchiest fry

Hi! I'm Besa. I'm 21 years old and hail from a small town near Cologne, Germany, where I attended school and spent most of my childhood playing video games, drawing anime characters, and making my own little websites and games with the RPG Maker. After graduating at 16 [humblebrag], I started studying Computer Science at RWTH Aachen University which also gave me the opportunity to get first experiences working for a tech start-up, and in my university's IT department. After four years of dabbling in different fields (mathematics and theoretical computer science still fascinate me) but not really getting anywhere, I enrolled in a "dual" program that would allow me to get on-the-job training while still working on my degree. So now I live in Mannheim where I attend Baden-Wuerttemberg Cooperative State University and work for an awesome IT company, learning to be software developer.

While my biggest passion will always be computer science, my other interests are pretty diverse. I love drawing, painting, and creating pixel art, making music by myself or with friends (I upload song covers on YouTube sometimes), sometimes taking out my camera for little photoshoots. Gaming is still a big hobby of mine—in late 2017, I joined Twitch and started streaming my games (mostly casual and retro) a couple times a week.

Indie game developers like ConcernedApe and Edmund McMillen greatly inspire me, and my goal is to be able to make a living with developing games someday. So right now I'm honing my programming and digital art skills, and I hope to learn some digital music production as well, so I can create my own games from the ground up, building them exactly the way I envisioned them. I'm also a huge proponent of free software (free as in liberty, not free as in beer—emphasis added for clarity). That's why I publish all my works under the GPLv3, meaning you're free to take and use parts or all of my code for whatever purpose you like, as long as the end product is licensed the same way. I think this is a great way to spread the free software movement— as more and more free apps and programs are released and more and more people use them and developers contribute, the quality continously increases and gets close to the level of their commercial/proprietary counterparts. I'm still waiting on major game developers to get the hint and start to release their games on Linux too, it's one of the last things holding me back from completely making the switch from Windows.
